Dal Khichdi

Description

Dal Khichdi is THE comfort dish for many Indians. It is made by cooking together rice and dal - that's what you call a Khichdi, followed by a delicious tadka which infuses the humble Khichdi with tons of new flavours and spices making it so delicious!

This recipe will show you to make dal khichdi with minimum hassle and you can easily relive comfort in a bowl! Servings: 2-3.

Ingredients

For the Khichdi
  • 1 cup rice
  • 1 cup tur dal
  • 1 tbsp turmeric
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1 tbsp peppercorns
  • salt to season
For the tadka
  • 3 tbsp ghee or clarified butter
  • 2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp asofoetida or hing
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 large tomato, diced
  • 10 green chillies
  • 1 stalk of curry leaves
  • 2 tsp ginger garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 1 tbsp turmeric
  • 1 tbsp garam masala
  • 2 cups water
  • salt as required
  • coriander to garnish

Steps

For the Khichdi
  1. Combine the rice and dal together and wash them till the water runs clear.
  2. Add the washed rice and dal along with the remaining ingredients to a pressure cooker.
  3. Cook on high flame till 4-5 whistles.
  4. After the Khichdi has cooked, let it sit for a few minutes to let steam escape.
  5. Then, safely open the presuure cooker and remove the bay leaf and cinnamon stick. Your Khichdi is ready!
For the tadka
  1. In a saucepan, begin by adding oil and heat it up.
  2. Once the oil has heated, add mustard seeds and cumin seeds.
  3. Add the chillies and curry leaves. Be careful of the oil splattering!
  4. After the splattering has reduced, add asofoetida and ginger garlic paste and let this cook for a minute ir two. Be sure to stir things around to avoid burning.
  5. Add the onions and saute them until they get translucent or slightly pink in color.
  6. Once the onions have cooked, add turmeric and garam masala. Let these spices cook for some time.
  7. We can stir in the tomatoes and green peas next, and then season with salt. We cook until the tomatoes have become mushy and turn off our flame.
Now we combine the two!
  1. In your Khichdi, stir in two cups of hot water.
  2. Whilst your tadka is still hot, we slowly add it to our now watered down Khichdi.
  3. Heat them up together, you can choose to add more water if required.
  4. After this cooked, we chop up some coriander and stir it in.
  5. To serve, pour your Dal Khichdi in a bowl, garnish with coriander. Serve with roasted papad. Your steaming bowl of comfort is ready!
